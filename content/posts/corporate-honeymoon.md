I'd often heard of the honeymoon phase for romantic relationships, but had yet to realise it's also evident in the early corporate lifestyle.

Be me, a fresh youth out of university. Get into the tech industry through Cisco. Honestly, I was astounded. I reached the first goal of a multi-phased life plan early - big tech.

It was beautiful... at first. I didn't have the traditional grad program due to starting months after my peers. I would go about my days doing whatever I wanted! Mainly, in that time, I focussed on my internetworking skillset for enterprise and security. I could already program, and, THEY ARE a networking vendor.

This went on for roughly five months, and during the time, I had someone approach me to solo dev a $2M IoT sales project from start to finish. I delivered it, proving myself. Even got to do presentations on it which was amazing experience!

Then I got put onto a full-time client, the honeymoon period ended:
- Meetings
- Meetings about meetings 
- Catch ups to talk about those meetings progress
- Business people talking about value
- Rote business value deliverables
- Clients taking weeks/months to respond to simple queries or just ignoring you to avoid work
- Clients not following your instructions and rawdogging problems to their own peril

I seriously cannot comprehend how someone can handle years of "where is the business value in doing this?". 

I have fallen out of love with my betrothed, but perhaps she was always this way? A behemoth of corporate lingo, outlooks and deliverables.

If anything, this new perspective has provided further kindling to ignite the fire of passion, freedom, and purpose. 

I yearn for more than the traditional corporate grindset.
