Return to Truth: CS + Math

  • Jan 13, 2024
  • 2 minute read

Consider this a letter to my future self. I will prove my previously held insecurities were simply a fallacy I tricked myself into believing.

TL;DR trends bad, CS and math understanding good.

The Problem

University was quite the subpar learning experience. Pseudo-CS classes with irrelevant material, rushed learning goals and timelines, dumbed-down material to bolster pass rates, among a lack of interest from a, larger than expected, percentage of tutors. This left gaps in my knowledge surrounding computing and CS.

The Why

Truthfully, I see myself as a self-taught engineer. This used to result in some insecurity, for a while an imagined badge of honour, I now see those sentiments as meaningless.

To reach the dreams I wish to attain, I require exceptionality with computers. I am self-aware enough to see that, at this moment, I am not so gud.

Furthermore, like many others, I previously held no purpose in pursuing higher mathematics. I see now that was a naive sentiment. Invention, beauty, the universe, all stem from this language.

There is a timeline where one chooses the hardest problems to tackle, another simply for money or the feeling of common satisfaction. I no longer see myself chasing after the latter.

The How

The books listed on teachyourselfcs.com seem to cover the weak points in my current understanding. Adding in related projects, my typical style of learning, will undoubtably further my development.

Math is infinitely more challenging, I honestly have no baseline for my knowledge. Hence I’ve decided to start from the beginning with arithmetic. I plan to utilise Khan Academy and the AOPS book series for this early stage.

Ultimately, I see this entire experience as a journey of growth, and have not set any stringent timelines on finishing books or concepts. As always, I shall read texts outside of these domains to expand my perspectives.
