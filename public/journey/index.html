Journey

Foreword

This page is for thoughts, achievements, and other meditations at certain points in the journey through life.

I stumbled across this idea whilst browsing the interwebs, and immediately decided to replicate it on my own site. Full credit to DeGatchi.

Career

2024

  • Endeavoured to read fiction again. Fantasy, Sci-fi, classics, why must you be so good?
  • Delved further into internetworking. MPLS, RIP, IS-IS, Service Provider and Enterprise network design.
  • Travelled to Japan (two weeks) to see mates. First solo travel. Ended up in Kumamoto eating horse sashimi and gallavanting with gyaru’s… would recommend.
  • Started taking adaptogens, highly noticeable results! A true elevation.

University 2020-2023

Ultimately, I have missed a lot from this section as many a long night were spent in the groove, simply going through the motions of initial learning curves. I duly credit the immersal state I was in to the rapid growth I had, and continue to have.

2023

  • Start the year off with a month-long internship at Big 4 Consulting. Regret time wasted on spreadsheets.
  • Evaluate my home cities tech situation. Dire indeed.
  • Capstone (cheating detection tool for academics) and a few other larger projects at Uni.
  • Start to develop curiosity for AI, hold myself back with insecurities surrounding math.
  • Graduate and get security job with Cisco Systems.

2022

  • Figure modern language is required for personal success, choose Python.
  • Begin building personal projects: port scanner; news scraper w/ email; website V1; small emulator in C; pitak - a pluggable RPi to launch network attacks from (ARP poisoning, DoS, DHCP starving).
  • Start learning about modern OPSEC and tech centralisation issues.
  • Daily drive Ubuntu, being a newb much mistakes made. Also Unix server stuff (networking, systemd, security).
  • Classes are ok, a lot of memorisation without much useful application.
  • Switch to Arch Linux.

2021

  • First interaction with programming is C. The single most difficult topic I had undertaken at the time.
  • Realise quite quickly textbooks, classes and tutors shan’t get me to the metaphorical finish line.
  • start_immersion.exe - jump into online forums, learn the history of computing, hacker culture, et cetera.
  • Become obsessed with computing and security; essentially respark the passion for learning I lost in high school.
  • A ton of unnecessary and uninformative classes later, finish first year.

2020

  • Graduate high school.
  • Interested in CybSec, decide to study it at local Uni.
  • Get job in retail (stayed till endish of 2022), dealt with a lot of bullshit.
